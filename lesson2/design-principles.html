Visual Hierarchy

Simply Chocolate
simplychocolate.com

Simply Chocolate does a great job of showing Visual Hierarchy. The way their page is set up allows the user to naviagte smoothly through the page. On the home page it takes the users attention and focuses it towards the product, and it does this without taking away from other elements on the screen. This same effortless navigation is present on every screen in the page. The result is a pleasent browsing experience witht the webpage almost guiding you through the website.

Fitts' Law

Circleci
circleci.com

Circleci is a good example of Fitts' Law because because I believe that it centers its call to action buttons and makes them the most prominent buttons on the screen. It tells the user "Hey clcik me!". Fitts' Law was designed to pull the users attention to certain points on the screen such as call to action buttons. I can see this website implementing that with their buttons and that is why I think this website is a good example of Fitts' Law.

White Space and Clean Design

Quip
getquip.com

Quip is a website that sells toothbrushes, and I believe it does a wonderful job at using White Space and Clean Design. On the home page you see pictures that rotate on behind the name of the sight, and these images explain what Quip is and what they sell. Thats it, all you need is the product name and the images behind it and you can tell exactly what this company is about and what they do. I think that is a great use of White Space and Clean Design. It's not cluttered and it gets its point across.
